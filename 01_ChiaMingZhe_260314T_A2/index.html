Video Editing

What is Video Editing?

Video editing is the process of selecting, arranging and improving video clips to create a clear and meaningful final product.

It can involve cutting out unnecessary parts, adding music, text, transitions, sound effects and visual effects.

However, video editing is not only about making a video look attractive.

A good editor also decides how information should be presented so that the audience can understand and stay interested in the message.

Why is this a somewhat essential lifeskill?

Video editing has become a somewhat essential life skill because videos are now widely used for communication, education and work.

Students may need to create video presentations for school projects, while employees may use videos for training, advertising or sharing information.

Knowing basic video editing skills allows a person to communicate ideas more clearly and creatively.

Learning video editing also develops useful skills such as creativity, organisation and critical thinking.
